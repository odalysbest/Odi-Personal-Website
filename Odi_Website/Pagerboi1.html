Pager for Communal Living

When living with 12 other people on a 15-acre property, tracking everyone down to let them know it's time for a meal or a meeting can feel more than a bit like herding cats. Rarely is everyone within earshot, let alone the same building. I decided to use my electronics skills and develop a pager system to help solve this problem, allowing anybody to alert people wherever they are on the farm.

A central transmitter unit is connected to wall power in the main house, with buttons denoting various meals and meetings. When one of the buttons is pressed, a signal is sent to any number of battery powered receiver units up to two kilometers away, which briefly beep and display a color on a screen corresponding to the meal or meeting type.

The transmitter and receiver units interface using 433MHz ZetaPlus RF Transciever modules in a unidirectional configuration. Both transmitter and receivers utilize an STM32L0 microprocessor to enable ultra low power consumption, maximizing battery life overall. The receiver's colored screen is driven by discrete RGB LEDs controlled by a TI LP5036 LED driver over I2C. The receiver's quiescent current is especially optimized, allowing for up to a month's operation off of a pair of rechargeable AA batteries.
