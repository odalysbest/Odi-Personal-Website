NASA-Funded Asteroid Discovery Research

Working with Dr. Carrie Nugent and helping them build a pipeline for discovering asteroids was one of the best college experiences I've had. Their research was centered around the NEAT dataset, an archive with thousands of images from different locations. With modern and sophisticated methods of image processing, we can discovery thousands of new asteroids. My work consisted of obtaining images from the NEAT dataset, running them through image processing algorithms, and calibrating their paramters on space software for better asteroid/star recognition. The software we used was source extractor, scamp, Aladin.

By fine tuning the algorithms, I was able to obtain images like the one above. I often ran into distortion and noise problems with the images based on their location/weather, One of my Python scripts divides images into quadrants, to make it easier for the image-processing to customize its parameters across the image. While it does not account for all distortion cases, it makes it easier for my successor to focus on star-removal algorithms and checking images with existing asteroid databases.
