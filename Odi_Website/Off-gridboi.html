Off-Grid Electrical System

When I took the opportunity to live off-the-grid for a semester, I knew power would be an issue. Arriving to the farm, I discovered the "power grid" consisted of a single low-efficiency solar panel and a gas generator. Over the course of the semester, I worked with other students to design and implement a robust electrical grid incorporating multiple power sources.

We calculated power requirements to support 15 people throughout a semester of remote learning. Between power-hungry laptops and kW-class corded drills, we were up against some steep requirements. We designed a power system incorporating a hetrogenous solar array for main power during the day, and a micro-hydro generator for 24/7 trickle charging. A large pure-sine inverter was used for main 120VAC power, boosting our overall efficiency (thanks, modern power electronics!).

Assembling and improving the grid was one of our main focuses over the semester, working through issues ranging from waterproofing to weak generator excitation fields to improving system efficiency and maintainability.
